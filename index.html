Instructions

Board and Pieces:

  • Dara is played on a 6 rows x 5 columns board.
  • Other dimensions are possible, such as 6 x 6.
  • Each of the 2 players has 12 pieces.
  • One player plays with black pieces, the other with white.
  • All 24 pieces are always visible, whether on the board or off.
  • Pieces off the board are grouped by color, one side for each.

Basic Rules:

  • Players choose a color and decide who plays first.
  • Initially, the board is empty.
  • The game has 2 phases:
  • Phase 1 - Placing the pieces (drop).
  • Phase 2 - Moving the pieces (move).

Pieces in Line:

  • Considered in line are: sequences of 3 or more contiguous pieces, horizontally or vertically (not diagonally), all of the same color.

Placing the Pieces:

  • Players alternately place one of their pieces in an empty house.
  • More than 3 pieces in a line cannot be placed.
  • 3 pieces in a line can exist (but it is not advantageous).

Moving the Pieces:

  • Each player alternately moves a piece of their color.
  • A piece can only be moved to an adjacent house.
  • Pieces are moved only horizontally or vertically (not diagonally).
  • A line of size 3 allows capturing an opponent's piece.
  • In this case, one piece of the opponent is removed at your choice.
  • Only one piece is removed per move (even if multiple lines are formed).
  • Lines with more than 3 pieces cannot be formed.
  • A piece cannot return to the position of the previous move.
  • It can only return to that house in subsequent moves.

End of the Game:

  • The game ends when a player cannot win.
  • For example, if there are only 2 pieces on the board.
  • Or if a valid move cannot be made.
  • The player who cannot win loses the game.
